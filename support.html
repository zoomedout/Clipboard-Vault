Getting Started

Up and running in three steps

1

Find Clipboard Vault in Your Menu Bar

After launching, look for the clipboard icon in your macOS menu bar at the top of the screen. Click it to open your clipboard history.

2

Grant Accessibility Permission

For the global hotkey (⌘⇧V) to work, enable Clipboard Vault in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility. Restart the app after granting.

3

Authenticate with Touch ID

Use Touch ID or your macOS password to unlock your encrypted clipboard history. You can configure the auto-lock delay in settings.

Frequently Asked Questions

QThe app doesn't appear in my Dock

Clipboard Vault also lives in your menu bar — look for the clipboard icon at the top of your screen for quick access.

QThe global hotkey isn't working

Make sure Clipboard Vault is enabled in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility. You may need to restart the app after granting permission.

QHow is my data protected?

All clipboard content is encrypted at rest using post-quantum hybrid encryption (X25519 + ML-KEM-768 with AES-256-GCM). Private keys live in the macOS Keychain. No data leaves your device.

QCan I recover deleted clips?

No. Once a clip is deleted, it's permanently removed. Pin important clips to protect them from auto-delete.

QWhere is my data stored?

All data is stored locally at ~/Library/Application Support/ClipboardVault/. Nothing is sent to any server.

QHow do I uninstall completely?

Delete the app from Applications. To remove all data, also delete ~/Library/Application Support/ClipboardVault/.

QDoes Clipboard Vault use any cloud services?

No. The app is completely offline. Zero network requests, zero analytics, zero tracking. Everything stays on your Mac.
