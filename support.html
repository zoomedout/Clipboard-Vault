Getting Started

Up and running in three steps

1

Open Clipboard Vault

Mac: Look for the clipboard icon in your menu bar at the top of the screen. Click it to open your clipboard history.
iPhone & iPad: Open the Clipboard Vault app from your Home Screen.

2

Authenticate with Touch ID or Face ID

Use Touch ID, Face ID, or your device password to unlock your encrypted clipboard history. You can configure the auto-lock delay in settings.

3

Sync Across Devices

Enable iCloud Sync in settings to keep your clipboard history in sync across your Mac, iPhone, and iPad. All data is encrypted end-to-end.

Frequently Asked Questions

QThe app doesn't appear in my Dock (Mac)

On Mac, Clipboard Vault lives in your menu bar — look for the clipboard icon at the top of your screen for quick access.

QHow is my data protected?

All clipboard content is encrypted at rest using post-quantum hybrid encryption (X25519 + ML-KEM-768 with AES-256-GCM). Each device has its own private keys that never leave the device. When iCloud Sync is enabled, data is re-encrypted with a separate AES-256 sync key for transport — so even if iCloud were compromised, your local data remains protected by device-specific keys.

QCan I recover deleted clips?

No. Once a clip is deleted, it's permanently removed. Pin important clips to protect them from auto-delete.

QWhere is my data stored?

Mac: All data is stored locally at ~/Library/Application Support/ClipboardVault/.
iPhone & iPad: Data is stored in the app's private container. Nothing is sent to any server unless iCloud Sync is enabled.

QHow do I uninstall completely?

Mac: Delete the app from Applications and remove ~/Library/Application Support/ClipboardVault/.
iPhone & iPad: Delete the app — all local data is removed automatically.

QDoes Clipboard Vault use any cloud services?

Only if you enable iCloud Sync — in that case, encrypted clipboard data syncs between your devices via iCloud. Otherwise, the app is completely offline. Zero analytics, zero tracking.
