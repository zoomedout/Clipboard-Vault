Under the Hood

The security architecture behind Clipboard Vault. Post-quantum cryptography, zero-knowledge design, and native performance — because your clipboard sees everything.

Post-Quantum Hybrid Encryption

Every encrypted clip is protected by two independent layers of cryptography: classical elliptic-curve (X25519) and quantum-resistant (ML-KEM-768), standardized by NIST in August 2024. If a future quantum computer breaks one layer, the other still protects your data. This is the same dual-layer strategy recommended by cryptographers worldwide for long-term data protection — and Clipboard Vault is one of the first apps of any kind to ship it.

// Anatomy of an encrypted clip
"v4:" + base64url(
  eph_pk[32]      // ephemeral X25519 public key
  | kem_ct[1088]   // ML-KEM-768 ciphertext (quantum-resistant)
  | nonce[12]     // AES-256-GCM nonce
  | ciphertext     // your data (variable length)
  | tag[16]       // authentication tag — tamper proof
)

Two Layers, One Guarantee

Every clip is encrypted with both X25519 (proven, battle-tested elliptic-curve cryptography) and ML-KEM-768 (the new quantum-resistant standard). An attacker would need to independently break both algorithms to access a single clip — a security margin no other clipboard manager offers.

ML-KEM-768 — NIST FIPS 203

Standardized by NIST in August 2024 after years of rigorous public evaluation. ML-KEM-768 provides 192-bit post-quantum security. Data you encrypt today stays protected even against quantum computers that do not yet exist — so your clipboard history is future-proof.

Per-Device Key Pairs

Each of your devices generates its own unique post-quantum encryption key pair for local storage, stored exclusively in the system Keychain. Private keys never leave your device — not to us, not to Apple, not to anyone. A separate AES-256 sync key is shared via iCloud Keychain only for CloudKit transport, keeping local and transit encryption fully independent.

AES-256-GCM Authenticated Encryption

Your data payload is sealed with AES-256-GCM — the same authenticated encryption that protects TLS connections, Signal messages, and virtually every serious security protocol in use today. The authentication tag means your data cannot be silently tampered with; any modification is detected and rejected.

Zero Knowledge

All encryption and decryption happens on your device. No key material is ever transmitted. There is no server we control, no escrow service, no recovery backdoor. We literally cannot read your data — and neither can anyone else. If you lose all your devices and your iCloud Keychain, your encrypted clips are mathematically unrecoverable. That is not a limitation; it is the point.

iCloud Sync Architecture

Your clipboard history syncs across all your Apple devices through iCloud — but with a two-key encryption architecture that treats iCloud as an untrusted transport. Each device encrypts locally with its own post-quantum key pair, while a shared AES-256 sync key protects data in transit through CloudKit. Even if iCloud were compromised, your local data remains protected by device-specific keys that never leave the device.

Two-Key Architecture

Local storage uses device-specific hybrid post-quantum keys. CloudKit transport uses an industry-standard AES-256-GCM symmetric sync key — the same approach used by Signal, iMessage, and other trusted encrypted services. Every clip is re-encrypted when pushed to CloudKit and again when pulled to a new device — iCloud never sees your data encrypted with your device's private key.

Your iCloud, Your Data

Sync runs through your personal iCloud account — not a server we control. Your clips are stored in a private container that only your devices can access. We have no server, no database, no account system, and no way to see what you have copied.

Fast, Incremental Sync

Only new or changed clips are synced — not your entire history every time. Push notifications trigger near-real-time updates, so a clip copied on your Mac appears on your iPhone within seconds without polling or manual refresh.

Everything Syncs

Text, images, files, pins, notes, and deletions all sync seamlessly. Delete a clip on one device and it disappears everywhere. Pin something on your iPhone and it is pinned on your Mac. Your clipboard history stays perfectly consistent across all your Apple devices.

Secure Key Distribution

The shared sync key is distributed between your devices through iCloud Keychain — Apple's hardware-backed, end-to-end encrypted key storage. Each device's local encryption keys never leave the device at all. The first device to enable sync generates the sync key; joining devices pull it automatically. No server, no intermediary, no exposure.

Full-Text Search Index

Find any clip from your history in milliseconds. The search index is loaded into memory for instant results and encrypted when saved to disk — so your search data gets the same protection as your clipboard data itself.

Instant Results

Start typing and results appear immediately. The search index is built in memory when you unlock the app, so queries resolve in milliseconds — no waiting, no loading spinners, no perceptible delay even with thousands of clips in your history.

Partial Word Matching

You do not need to remember the exact word. Searching for board finds clipboard. Searching for rypt finds encrypted. Every substring is searchable, because the thing you are looking for is always the one you half-remember.

Encrypted at Rest

The search index is encrypted with the same post-quantum hybrid encryption used for your clips. When you lock the app, the in-memory index is wiped completely. When you unlock, it is restored from the encrypted copy on disk. Your search history never exists unprotected.

Notes Are Searchable Too

Add a note to any clip and it becomes part of the search index. Search for a keyword in your note and the attached clip surfaces instantly. Annotate clips with project names, context, or reminders — then find them by those annotations later.

Content Types & Formats

Text, images, and files — Clipboard Vault captures and encrypts everything you copy. Each content type is handled with purpose-built encryption and storage, and smart detection automatically classifies what you have copied so you can find it later.

Text

Plain text, rich text, code snippets, URLs, and more. Optionally preserve formatting so pasting into a document keeps your bold, italic, and links intact. Every text clip is individually encrypted before being stored.

Images

Screenshots, photos, diagrams — PNG, JPEG, HEIC, GIF, WebP, TIFF, and BMP are all supported. Images are encrypted and stored on disk with thumbnail previews generated for browsing, so your history loads fast without compromising security.

Videos

Share videos from Photos or copy video files — MP4, MOV, and more. Videos are stream-encrypted in 1 MB chunks for constant memory usage. Thumbnail previews are generated automatically, and tap to play via QuickLook. Files up to 200 MB are supported.

Files

Any file type — PDFs, documents, archives, code files. Large files are encrypted using a streaming approach, processing in 1 MB chunks without consuming excessive memory. Files up to 200 MB are supported. Copy multiple files at once on macOS or multi-select in the iOS Share Extension.

Link Previews

URLs are detected and displayed with visual link preview styling in your history. Importantly, no remote metadata is fetched — your clipboard activity is never leaked to third-party servers. The preview is generated entirely from the URL itself, keeping your browsing habits completely private.

Biometric Authentication

Clipboard Vault locks itself when you close it and requires biometric authentication to re-open. Touch ID on Mac, Face ID on iPhone and iPad. Your clipboard history is never visible without you — and never decrypted in memory while locked.

Touch ID & Face ID

Authentication uses the native system biometric prompt — the same one you see when unlocking your Mac or authorizing Apple Pay. No custom dialogs, no workarounds. If your device has a biometric sensor, Clipboard Vault uses it.

Password Fallback

If biometrics fail or are unavailable, you can authenticate with your macOS login password directly within the app. A secure text field appears inline — no external windows, no disruption to your workflow.

Configurable Lock Delay

Choose how quickly Clipboard Vault locks after you close it. Options range from immediate (the default) to 5 minutes, so you can balance security and convenience. Immediate lock means re-authentication every time you open the app.

Memory Protection

When the app locks, decryption keys are wiped from memory and the search index is cleared. No decrypted content remains resident in RAM. When you unlock, keys are securely re-derived from the Keychain and your search index is rebuilt from its encrypted copy.

Storage & Retention

Your clipboard history is stored locally with configurable retention policies. You decide how long clips are kept and which content types are tracked — the app handles the rest. The retention setting syncs across all your devices via iCloud.

Shared Across Platforms

The same database format is used on macOS and iOS. Your clipboard history is structured identically on both platforms, so synced data arrives ready to use with no conversion or compatibility issues.

Configurable Retention

Set retention from 1 hour to forever. Expired clips are automatically cleaned up on launch and after each new copy. This setting syncs across all your devices via iCloud, so you only configure it once.

Per-Type Tracking

Toggle tracking independently for text, images, and files. Toggle encryption independently for each type too. Disable image tracking if you only care about text, or turn off file tracking to save disk space.

Automatic Cleanup

Clips older than your retention window are automatically removed on launch and after each new copy. Pinned clips are always exempt — they stay until you delete them. You can also manually clear history by time range: last hour, last 24 hours, last 7 days, last 30 days, or all time.

Recently Deleted

Accidentally deleted a clip? No problem. Deleted clips are moved to Recently Deleted, where you can recover them or permanently remove them. Like the Photos app — but for your clipboard.

Recover Anytime

Deleted clips are kept in Recently Deleted so you can restore them if you change your mind. Recover a single clip or select multiple at once to restore in bulk.

Synced Across Devices

Delete a clip on your Mac and it moves to Recently Deleted on your iPhone too. Recover it on any device and it comes back everywhere. Your trash stays in sync just like your history.

Permanent Delete

When you are sure, permanently delete clips from Recently Deleted. Or choose to skip the trash entirely — a setting lets you control whether deletes go to Recently Deleted or are immediate.

Searchable

Recently Deleted clips are still searchable. Search your full history and trashed clips appear with a clear label, so you can find and recover something even if you forgot you deleted it.

Multi-Select & Bulk Actions

Select multiple clips at once and act on them all together. Copy, delete, share, or pin in bulk — on both Mac and iOS.

Flexible Selection

On Mac, Cmd+click to pick individual clips or Shift+click for a range. On iPhone and iPad, tap Select and check the clips you want. Select All is always one tap away.

Bulk Copy & Share

Copy multiple clips to your clipboard at once, or share them via the system share sheet. Images, files, and text — all supported in bulk.

Bulk Delete & Pin

Clean up your history by deleting multiple clips at once. Or pin a batch of important clips to the top in one action. Works in Recently Deleted too — recover or permanently delete in bulk.

Works Everywhere

Multi-select works the same way in your main history and in Recently Deleted. Keyboard shortcuts on Mac, familiar edit mode on iOS — consistent across both platforms.

Privacy by Design

No accounts. No telemetry. No third-party SDKs. No server. Clipboard Vault is a local-first app that optionally syncs through your own iCloud account. We never see your data because we have no infrastructure to see it with.

No Accounts, No Cloud We Control

There is no sign-up, no login, no backend server. If you enable sync, it goes through your personal iCloud account — your private container, accessible only by your own devices. We have no server, no database, and no ability to access your data.

Encryption Keys Never Leave Your Devices

Your local encryption keys are stored in the system Keychain — Apple's hardware-backed secure storage. Private keys are never written to disk as files, never logged, and never included in unencrypted backups. The sync key is distributed between your devices through iCloud Keychain's end-to-end encrypted channel, but your device-specific private keys never leave the device.

No Analytics or Tracking

Zero analytics frameworks. Zero tracking pixels. Zero network requests except iCloud sync (when you enable it). The app makes no outbound connections to any server we operate — because we do not operate any. Your clipboard activity is nobody's business.

App Store Sandboxed

Clipboard Vault runs in the macOS and iOS sandbox with minimal entitlements. No background network access. No access to your contacts, calendar, location, or microphone. The app can only do what it needs to do: read your clipboard, encrypt it, and store it locally.

Native Performance

Built entirely in Swift with no web views, no Electron, no cross-platform compromises. Clipboard Vault is a true native app on both macOS and iOS — fast to launch, light on resources, and respectful of your battery.

100% Native

No embedded browser, no JavaScript runtime, no bridging layers. The UI is built with Apple's own frameworks on both platforms. Interface interactions respond in single-digit milliseconds — the kind of speed you only get from native code.

Concurrency Without Compromise

The clipboard monitor, encryption engine, search index, and sync system all run concurrently using Swift's modern concurrency model. New clips are captured, encrypted, and indexed without ever blocking the UI or causing lag.

Lives in Your Menu Bar

On macOS, Clipboard Vault runs quietly in the menu bar — no Dock icon, no app switcher clutter. A global hotkey gives you instant access to your history. The panel appears and disappears without disrupting whatever you are working on.

Efficient Clipboard Monitoring

The clipboard is checked with sub-50ms polling, but new content is only processed when something actually changes. Smart self-copy detection prevents the app from recording its own paste operations, keeping your history clean and relevant.

Minimal Footprint

Two libraries total — one for the database, one for post-quantum cryptography. No bloated dependency trees, no unnecessary frameworks. All encryption is handled by Apple's CryptoKit and a statically linked quantum-resistant library. The result is a lean, fast binary with a minimal attack surface.
