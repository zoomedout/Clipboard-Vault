Your clipboard sees everything — passwords, photos, code, messages. Here's how Clipboard Vault keeps it all safe, organized, and instantly accessible.

Security

Your clipboard sees everything. We make sure no one else does.

We don't recommend copying passwords or API keys into any clipboard manager — but life happens. You copy a password from your email. You grab a 2FA code. A coworker sends you credentials in Slack. Most clipboard apps store all of that in plaintext.

Clipboard Vault encrypts everything the moment it's copied — with post-quantum cryptography that's built to withstand even future quantum computers. Your history is locked behind Touch ID on Mac and Face ID on iPhone. So even if sensitive data ends up on your clipboard, it's encrypted before it hits the disk.

File Transfer

Remember emailing files to yourself?

You're editing a document on your Mac and need it on your phone. With AirDrop, you open the share sheet, wait for your phone to appear, accept the transfer, find the file. With iCloud Drive, you open Finder, navigate to the folder, then open Files on your phone and wait for it to download.

With Clipboard Vault: you copy. That's it. Open the app on your phone — it's already there. Photos from your phone show up on your Mac the same way. No share sheets, no "waiting for device," no cables, no emailing files to yourself.
