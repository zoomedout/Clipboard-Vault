Last updated: March 8, 2026

Overview

Clipboard Vault is a clipboard manager for Mac, iPhone, and iPad, developed by Arjun Donthala. Your data stays on your device unless you enable iCloud Sync, in which case it is encrypted end-to-end between your devices.

Data Stored Locally

The following data is stored on your device and nowhere else:

  • Clipboard history — text, images, files, and RTF content you copy
  • Notes you attach to clipboard items
  • App settings and preferences
  • Encryption keys (in your device Keychain and iCloud Keychain for sync)

Storage Location

Mac: ~/Library/Application Support/ClipboardVault/
iPhone & iPad: The app's private container (not accessible by other apps).

Encryption

All clipboard content is encrypted at rest using post-quantum hybrid encryption with device-specific keys. When syncing, data is re-encrypted with a shared AES-256-GCM sync key for CloudKit transport — your device-specific private keys never leave the device.

  • X25519 — elliptic curve key agreement (local encryption)
  • ML-KEM-768 — post-quantum key encapsulation, NIST FIPS 203 (local encryption)
  • HKDF-SHA256 — key derivation (local encryption)
  • AES-256-GCM — authenticated encryption (local and sync transport)

Local private keys are stored in the device Keychain, protected by the Secure Enclave where available. The sync key is shared between your devices via iCloud Keychain.

Authentication

Clipboard Vault uses Touch ID, Face ID, or device password authentication to protect access to your history. Authentication is handled entirely by the operating system on your device. No credentials are transmitted or stored by the app.

Third-Party Services

Clipboard Vault uses no third-party analytics, tracking, or advertising. If you enable iCloud Sync, encrypted data is synced via Apple's CloudKit. No other network requests are made by the app.

Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or transfer any user data to any third party. There is no data to share — everything stays on your device.

Data Deletion

You can delete individual items within the app, or remove all data by deleting:

~/Library/Application Support/ClipboardVault/

Children's Privacy

The app does not collect any data from anyone, including children under 13.
